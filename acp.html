The Mystery of Cats' Purring!

Cats purr for various reasons, from contentment to stress. Interestingly, cats can also purr when they are injured, and some experts believe it may have a healing effect. It has been suggested that the frequency of a cat's purring might promote bone density and healing, which is an incredible function of this soothing sound.

Cats are independent, but they still love affection! They have been domesticated for thousands of years, with the earliest evidence showing humans and cats coexisting in ancient Egypt. Unlike dogs, cats were likely domesticated by their own choice, attracted by the abundance of food around human settlements.
